Quick Reference

  • Lights

    At night, bicycles must be equipped with a white front light visible at 500 feet and a rear red reflector (or rear red light visible at 200 feet). Buy some lights today...
    Chicago: 9-52-080 (a)

  • Brakes

    Your bicycle must have a brake that can make the bicycle skid.
    MCC: 9-52-080 (b)

  • Parking

    You can park your bike on any sign pole, bike rack, or parking meter. You can also park against a curb or building "in such manner as to afford the least obstruction to pedestrian traffic".
    MCC: 9-52-070

  • Sidewalks

    Don't bike on the sidewalks unless you're 11 or younger or it's a marked bike route.

    Chicago: 9-52-020

  • Turn Signals

    Make a turn signal at least 100 feet before you intend to turn. Extend your left arm to indicate a left turn; extend your right arm to indicate a right turn.
    ILCS: 11-15-1511, 11-8-806

  • Take The Lane

    You can ride in the center of a lane when the path is blocked, unsafe, or when there's not enough room for automobile drivers to safely pass you.
    ILCS: 11-15-1505

  • Ride With Traffic

    Don't ride against traffic, including on one-way streets. There are some instances where you can ride against traffic on a one-way street but these are clearly marked.
    ILCS: 11-15-1502

  • Signs and Signals

    Bicyclists must obey all traffic signs and signals on the road (except where bicycles are explicitly excepted).
    ILCS: 11-15-1502

  • Doorings

    Avoid getting doored – running into or getting hit by a car door – by riding 4 feet away from parked cars. It's illegal to open a car door into moving traffic. If you get doored, consider it a crash and follow the procedures in Crashed? Do This. If you are doored by a taxi passenger, they are at fault, but collect everyone's information.

    Chicago: 9-80-035

  • 3 Feet Passing

    Not for bicycling: automobile drivers must pass you at least 3 feet away. In many crashes, this is the minimum citation the driver will receive.
    MCC: 9-36-010 (c)

  • Left Turns

    There are two ways to turn left: merge into the left-most lane and turn from there; or, make a box turn by proceeding across the intersection, reorienting your bike towards the left (in the cross direction), and going straight after obeying traffic signals.
    ILCS: 11-15-1510

  • Drinking and Cycling

    You can't get a DUI*, but you can still get citations for being intoxicated in public or having an open alcohol container. * DUIs can only be given to "vehicle" operators and a bicycle is a "device", not a "vehicle".
